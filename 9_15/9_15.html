Examining a System with Negative Feedback Regulation


\[ U \ce{->[\alpha_u] U+m} \\ \ce{m ->[\sigma] m + x} \\ \ce{x^2 -> x_2} \\ \ce{x_2 -> \phi}; \quad \ce{x -> \phi}; \quad \ce{m -> \phi} \\ \dot{m} = \alpha_u U - \gamma_m m \dot{x} = \sigma m - \gamma_1 x - 2 k_d x^2 + 2k_{-d}x_2+k_{-b}B-\gamma_2 x_2 \\ \dot{U} = -k_b x_2 U + k_{-b}B \\ \dot{B} = k_b x_2 U - k_-b B \\ D_0 = U + B \]

Using the QSSA:

\[ k_d x^2 = k_{-d}x_2 => x_2 = \frac{k_d}{k_{-d}} x^2 \\ k_b x_2 U = k_{-b} B = k_{-b} (D_0 - U) \\ U = \frac{D_0}{1+(x/c)^2}; \quad c = \sqrt{\frac{k_{-b} k_{-d}}{k_b k_d}} \\ m = \alpha_u U/\gamma_m = \frac{\alpha_u D_0/\gamma_m}{1+(x/c)^2} \\ \alpha = \frac{\sigma \alpha_u D_0}{\gamma_m} \\ \dot{x} = \frac{\alpha}{(1+(x/c)^2)} - \gamma_1 x \]

Fixing Problems with the QSSA:

We want to avoid the problems of mixed manifolds. Instead of examining dynamics of x which is both fast and slow, instead look at...

\[ T = x + 2 x_2 + 2B \]

This is total concentration of x in any form. Therefore...

\[ \dot{T} = \sigma m - \gamma_1 x - 2 \gamma_2 x_2 \\ T = x + 2 \frac{k_d}{k_{-d}} x^2 + \frac{2 D_0 (x/c)^2}{1+(x/2)^2} \]

If \(D_0\) is much less than x...

\[ T = x + 2 \frac{k_d}{k_{-d}} x^2 \\ \dot{T} = \frac{dT}{dx} \frac{dx}{dt} \\ \frac{dT}{dx} = 1 + 4 \frac{k_d}{k_{-d}} x \\ (1 + 4 \frac{k_d}{k_{-d}} x) \dot{x} = \sigma m - \gamma_1 x - 2 \gamma_2 x_2 \\ (1 + 4 \frac{k_d}{k_{-d}} x) \dot{x} = \frac{\alpha}{1+(x/c)^2} - \gamma_1 x - 2 \gamma_2 \frac{k_d}{k_{-d}} x^2 \]

When feedback is involved, one should use "pQSSA" or "pre-factor QSSA". In general:

  1. Write down total amount of protein (T) as a function of the monomer, using equilibrium of fast reactions.
  2. Get T(X) and find \(\frac{dT}{dx}\)
  3. Find the slow rxns that create or destroy protein.
    \[\dot{T} = (prod) - (degradation)\]
  4. Then we find: \[ \frac{dT}{dx} \dot{x} = (prod) - (degradation) \]

Proteolysis

\[ \ce{P + E <=>[k_1][k_2] E' ->[\delta] E} \]

Assuming that E' is in equilibrium

\[ \dot{E'} = k_1 P E - k_2 E' - \delta E' \\ E_{tot} = E + E' \\ k_1 P E = k_2 E' + \delta E' \\ k_1 P [E_tot - E'] = k_2 E' + \delta E' \\ E' = \frac{P E_{tot}}{(\frac{k_2 + \delta}{k_1}) + P} \\ \dot{P} = -\delta E' = -\frac{\delta E_{tot} P}{\frac{k_2 + \delta}{k_1} + P} = -\frac{V_{Max} P}{K_m + p} \\ \dot{P} = f'(P) - \gamma P -\frac{V_{Max} P}{K_m + P} \]
